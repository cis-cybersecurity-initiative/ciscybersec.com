Introduction to Personal OpSec: Staying Safe in the Digital Playground
Mattis Stene-JohansenJan 9, 2019 at 7:38 pm
share

With ubiquitous internet connections in most places of the world, the total amount of internet users have surpassed 4 billion. Yes, that is 4 000 000 000 individuals. This is about 53% of the total world population. With this in mind, it should be clear that a lot of people are exposed to internet-related cybercrimes.

Our online security can be divided into two categories; enterprise OpSec and personal Opsec. OpSec stands for Operational Security, and is "the action of protecting information which might be used against one in a hostile encounter". We, as consumers, cannot control what enterprises does with our security. What we, as responsible internet users, can do is part of personal SecOps. Personal security operations include everything you do to keep yourself safe and sound surfing the cyberspace for pictures of kittens.

The very basics of personal security operations online comes down to a simple principle: habits. In order to succesfully secure yourself, you must make it a habit to do so. Let's look at an example: it is said that it is important to update your passwords regularily, but it is a tedious task and easy to skip. It is therefore important to establish a healthy habit of changing passwords. Another example is using a VPN (See link-to-vpn-intro), where it should become a habit to connect to it every time you're going to surf the web. An easy rule of thumb is to connect to your VPN every time you open your computer/device.
